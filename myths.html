• Legend of the Samurai

Myths


As a figure that exists only in legend today, the samurai are surrounded by many stories - fact or fiction. In popular culture, the Samurai have been a staple on Japanese movies and TV. Films such as The Seven Samurai, Yojimbo, and more recently 47 Ronin depict these warriors in ways that we can't be sure are truthful depictions or not. Fortunately, the underlying historical evidence can point us in the right direction if we choose to debunk some of these myths and theories.

Common Myths
A disgraced samurai could regain his honour through sempuku (suicide)

While this has occurred several times historically, the samurai where also bound to social rules and so this would not generally occur except under the most humiliating loss of honour. It does however frequently appear in the samurai code of honour.

Samurai would use the blade of their swords to block an enemy attack

This is true, however blocking with the blade could cause irreversible damage to it. It was preferred to instead sidestep, and launch a counter attack if possible. The side of the blade was preferred for blocking over the cutting edge.

Samurai's swords where composed of steel folded thousands of times

True! High quality katana have been known to have been folded up to 32,000 times.

Sometimes the samurai would use two swords in battle

True! Popularized by Miyamoto Musashi, dual wielding swords in battle was occasionally attempted by the samurai. Typically, one sword would be a full length katana while the other would be a wakizashi or o-wakizashi. This style is refered to as nitochi, or "two swords as one."
