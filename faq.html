I want to buy a training sword, whats the best one?

For practising striking techniques by yourself I recommend a bokken made of red oak or another hardwood and around 2.5 to 3lbs to closely mimic the weight of a metal katana. Make sure the tsuka ito (grip) is wrapped firm and not just glued or it will decay fast. The kashira (pommel) should stick out enough that you can feel it so you don't need to look to align your grip. If you are into sparring, I recommend cold steel's polypropylene training bokken. Its very flexible and almost impossible to break, unlike the wooden versions. Please be careful sparring if you are not trained, I suggest attending a reputable martial arts school and finding someone to teach you. Even training swords can cause bad injuries.
